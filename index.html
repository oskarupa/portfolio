Professional Identity

As an Industrial Designer, I'm interested in creating meaningful products that combine interaction of the physical and digital world. I bring playfulness, creativity, and the ability to realize the technology of my projects.

I look at design through the lens of playful interactions, I believe games hold core principles that apply to all interactive experiences. Especially products that combine physical and digital space.

This year, I expanded my technical toolkit beyond Unity into Generative AI. I learnt to treat code and data as raw materials for design. My goal for the rest of my studies is to evolve from a maker into a Balanced Designer. I will apply my digital craftsmanship to physical products based on user needs and contexts.

Manifesto for engaging interactive experiences

My vision is to design for a future where interaction with the virtual world is seamless, and empowers people to connect, create, and explore.

01. CREATIVITY AS A CORE FEATURE

Great experiences allow experimentation, building and creative expression. When creating is a part of the experience, users collaborate and share their creations with others.

02. FUN BEFORE LEARNING

A memorable experience is always playful, even if it has a serious purpose. I want to create experiences that teach users due of their curiosity, not because they should.

03. DESIGN FOR HUMAN CONNECTION

It could be cooperative, competitive or social. Technology should bring people together by motivating real world interaction.

Pointless

Pointless was created as part of Project 2, where the assignment was to create an interactive companion. Our goal was to experiment with 'unnatural' methods of communicating emotions.

The tetrahedron communicates it's state using it's shape changing abilities. Based on the proximity of the user's hand, Pointless will change the aggressiveness and direction of it's movements.

Pointless reacts to the proximity of the human hand and uses an array of servos to individually control triangular faces. My coding and creative electronics abilities greatly influenced the outcome of the prototype. I believe the final result is a great example of a strong interactive experience.

The game allows multiple players to play on the same device for an added level of difficulty and competition. There can be a maximum of three snakes â€“ where one is controlled by the WASD keys, one is controlled by the arrow keys, and one is controlled by a Joystick connected to an Arduino.

Memory Box

Memory Box is a digital and physical educational game that brings families together to expand on the storytelling experience. The design includes a wooden box with a translucent top. Included are transparent elements, that the players use to visually demonstrate their stories. The box is designed to house a phone camera that records the story from below.

For me, Memory Box is an powerful project that demonstrated how an interactive game can use creativity to promote social connections that matter.

During user testing, we found that creating a visual while telling a story makes it easier to understand and to tell.
